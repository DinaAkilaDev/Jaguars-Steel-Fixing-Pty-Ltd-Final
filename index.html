Our Services

We Provide Services

Raft slabs consist of a thick steel reinforced slab that is integrated with steel reinforced beams, which are dug into the ground for added strength and support. The depth and spacing of the edge and stiffening beams is dictated by the ground it is being constructed on.

Raft Slabs

+

Foundations provide support for structures, transferring their load to layers of soil or rock that have sufficient bearing capacity and suitable settlement characteristics.

Strip Footings

+

The base is bolted to a large concrete pad that supports the crane. The base connects to the mast (or tower), which gives the tower crane its height.

Crane Base

+

Ground slabs are those slabs that are poured directly into excavated trenches in the ground. They rely entirely on the existing ground for support. The ground (more correctly known in the industry as the foundation) must be strong enough to support the concrete slab

Ground Slabs

+

Suspended slabs are slabs that are not in direct contact with the ground. They form roofs or floors above ground level. Suspended slabs are grouped into two types: one way slabs which are supported on two sides. two way slabs which are supported on all four sides.

Suspended Slabs

+

Pad footings are an integral part of any of large construction project. Whether it be big or small, our crews are capable of organizing and laying all types of pad footings. Typically the process is the footings get dug then steel is ordered. We then arrive with our crew and organize a good approach to lay the steel in your footings.
